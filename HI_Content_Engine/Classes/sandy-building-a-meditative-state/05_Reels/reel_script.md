## Purpose
Short-form teaching recap built directly from the class transcript.

## Script Beats
- Hook: A meditative state is not something you force. It is something you build.
- Transcript pull: Begin by finding a steady seat and letting the base of the posture feel grounded.
- Practice note: As you inhale, feel length through the sides of the body and a quiet lift through the spine.
- Practice note: Instead, let the sternum broaden gently while the shoulders soften away from the ears.
- Close: Save this and return to it before your next practice.

## Transcript Evidence
- Begin by finding a steady seat and letting the base of the posture feel grounded.
- When the base is stable, the breath has somewhere to land.
- Over time the posture becomes a place of listening, and the breath becomes a guide instead of a task.
- This is how we begin to build a meditative state.
- If the breath becomes hard, you have gone a little too far.
- Today we are not trying to force the mind into meditation.
