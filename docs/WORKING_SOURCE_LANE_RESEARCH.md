# Working Source Lane Research

This document records researched temporary working source candidates for areas where the app needs stronger practical outcome logic before the final user-supplied books are available.

These are not replacements for `docs/CORE_BOOK_CANON.md`. They are internal scaffolding candidates only. Do not expose them in app results as "placeholder" books, and do not ingest or commit copyrighted full text unless lawful access or user-provided private access is confirmed.

## Current Knowledge Gaps

The app currently needs stronger practical source support in these areas:

1. TCM named herbs, formula categories, and formula strategy.
2. Ayurveda clinical assessment, treatment management, diet/herb/practice organization.
3. Homeopathy remedy confirmation, modern repertory navigation, and case refinement.
4. General herb cross-checking and broad safety context.
5. Case-taking, communication, and user-facing explanation.

## Recommended Working Source Candidates

### 1. TCM Herb Materia Medica

Primary candidate:

- `Chinese Herbal Medicine: Materia Medica (Portable 3rd Edition)`
- Authors: Dan Bensky, Steve Clavey, Erich Stoger, with Andrew Gamble.
- Publisher/source page: Eastland Press.
- App use: TCM herb properties, temperature/taste/channel/action categories, herb family differentiation, contraindication review, and narrowing broad herb categories.
- Research basis: Eastland describes it as a professional reference covering more than 530 commonly used herbs in the Chinese pharmacopoeia.
- URL: https://www.eastlandpress.com/products/chinese-herbal-medicine-materia-medica-portable-3rd-ed

Secondary candidate:

- `Chinese Medical Herbology and Pharmacology`
- Authors: John K. Chen and Tina T. Chen.
- Publisher/source page: Art of Medicine Press.
- App use: herb-drug interaction awareness, pharmacology notes, toxicology/safety index, and modern safety cross-checking.
- Research basis: Art of Medicine Press describes the book as including traditional uses, dosages/preparation, pharmacological effects, clinical studies/research, herb-drug interactions, safety index, toxicology, and overdose symptoms/treatments.
- URL: https://www.aompress.com/book-herbology

Recommendation:

- Use Bensky/Clavey/Stoger as the first TCM materia medica working lane.
- Use Chen/Chen as a secondary safety/pharmacology cross-check lane, especially when the app needs herb-drug interaction caution.

### 2. TCM Formulas

Primary candidate:

- `Chinese Herbal Medicine: Formulas & Strategies (Portable 2nd Edition)`
- Authors: Volker Scheid, Dan Bensky, Andrew Ellis, Randall Barolet.
- Publisher/source page: Eastland Press.
- App use: formula category organization, formula strategy, modifications, pattern-to-formula mapping, and formula family comparison.
- Research basis: Eastland describes the text as including more than 800 formulas from classical and modern sources, historical context, preparation/use pointers, formula categories, appendices, cross references, and indices.
- URL: https://www.eastlandpress.com/products/chinese-herbal-medicine-formulas-strategies-portable-2nd-edition

Secondary candidate:

- `Chinese Herbal Formulas and Applications`
- Authors: John K. Chen and Tina T. Chen.
- Publisher/source page: Art of Medicine Press.
- App use: formula applications, contemporary research links, formula safety, and herb-drug interaction support.
- Research basis: Art of Medicine Press presents it as bridging historical foundations and contemporary research/application of traditional Chinese herbal medicine.
- URL: https://www.aompress.com/book-formulas

Recommendation:

- Use Bensky/Scheid/Ellis/Barolet as the first formula strategy lane.
- Use Chen/Chen as a secondary safety/application lane.

### 3. Ayurveda Assessment And Treatment Direction

Primary candidates already in the core canon:

- `Textbook of Ayurveda, Vol. 2: A Complete Guide to Clinical Assessment`
- Author: Vasant Lad.
- Publisher/source page: The Ayurvedic Institute.
- App use: prakriti/vikriti, agni, ama, dhatu/mala, pulse/tongue/clinical observation, assessment structure, and intake logic.
- Research basis: The Ayurvedic Institute describes Volume 2 as providing a systematic method for identifying causes, stages of disease development, and assessment using Ayurvedic and modern techniques.
- URL: https://ayurveda.com/ayurvedic-press/volume-2-textbook-of-ayurveda-a-complete-guide-to-clinical-assessment/

- `Textbook of Ayurveda, Vol. 3: General Principles of Management and Treatment`
- Author: Vasant Lad.
- App use: treatment categories, management logic, diet/lifestyle/herb/practice organization.
- Access status: keep as private/local or metadata-only unless lawful access or user-provided copy is available.

Concise practical candidate:

- `Ayurvedic Medicine: The Principles of Traditional Practice`
- Author: Sebastian Pole.
- Publisher/catalog sources: NLM and Google Books list Churchill Livingstone / Elsevier Health Sciences editions.
- App use: concise treatment strategies, traditional herbal pharmacy/pharmacology, practical bridge language for modern complementary health practitioners.
- Research basis: NLM lists the book with MeSH headings for Ayurvedic medicine and medicinal plants; Google Books describes it as covering Ayurveda principles, treatment strategies, and traditional Ayurvedic herbal pharmacy/pharmacology.
- URLs:
  - https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nlmcatalog/101286245
  - https://books.google.com/books/about/Ayurvedic_Medicine.html?id=wH2C3PuOHzYC

Recommendation:

- Use Lad Vol. 2 for assessment architecture.
- Use Lad Vol. 3 and Sebastian Pole for practical treatment-category language.
- Keep Svoboda's `Prakriti` for constitution/philosophy, not primary herb/formula output.

### 4. Homeopathy Remedy Confirmation And Case Refinement

Primary working candidates already in the core canon:

- `Desktop Guide to Keynotes and Confirmatory Symptoms`
- Author: Roger Morrison.
- Publisher/catalog sources: NLM, Google Books, HomeopathicBooks.
- App use: keynotes, confirmatory symptoms, remedy confirmation, similar-remedy comparison, concise modern language.
- Research basis: NLM records it as a materia medica handbook; HomeopathicBooks describes it as covering about 290 remedies, organized with general description, mental symptoms, general symptoms, body parts, clinical section, combined symptoms, similar remedies, and an alphabetical symptom index.
- URLs:
  - https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nlmcatalog/100958852
  - https://www.homeopathicbooks.com/product/desktop-guide-to-keynotes-roger-morrison/

- `Desktop Companion to Physical Pathology`
- Author: Roger Morrison.
- App use: pathology-context remedy differentials and physical complaint refinement.
- Access status: private/local or metadata-only until lawful source/copy is supplied.

- `Homeopathic Medical Repertory`, 3rd ed.
- Author: Robin Murphy.
- App use: modern clinical repertory, organ-system/clinical-diagnosis bridge, rubric navigation.
- Research basis: Homeobook review describes the third edition as containing chapters in alphabetical order, homeopathic references, remedy list, and word index.
- URL: https://www.homeobook.com/book-review-on-homoeopathic-medical-repertory-3rd-edition/

- `The Science of Homeopathy`
- Author: George Vithoulkas.
- Publisher/catalog sources: NLM and International Academy of Classical Homeopathy.
- App use: homeopathy framework, levels of health, case reasoning, diagnosis/administration/evaluation methodology.
- Research basis: NLM lists the 1980 Grove Press edition; Vithoulkas' academy describes the book as covering principles and clinical applications, including practical application, diagnosis, preparation, administration, and evaluation.
- URLs:
  - https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nlmcatalog/8007054
  - https://www.vithoulkas.com/product/science-homeopathy/

- `The Soul of Remedies`
- Author: Rajan Sankaran.
- Publisher/source page: Rajan Sankaran official site.
- App use: remedy essence/sensation language and deeper psychological remedy patterning.
- Research basis: Sankaran's page frames it as a companion to earlier works and a remedy-focused practical text.
- URL: https://rajansankaran.com/product/the-soul-of-remedies/

Additional possible working candidate:

- `The Complete Repertory`
- Author: Roger van Zandvoort.
- App use: expanded repertory/rubric navigation after Kent/Murphy are stable.
- Research basis: Open Library lists a 1996 edition; Complete Dynamics describes the project as covering many remedies/rubrics and emphasizing clinical confirmations and source references.
- URLs:
  - https://openlibrary.org/books/OL19441963M/The_complete_repertory
  - https://www.completedynamics.com/pages/options/info_cr2021_en.html

Recommendation:

- Keep Morrison + Murphy + Kent as the first practical homeopathy refinement lane.
- Use Vithoulkas and Sankaran as framework/interpretive layers, not as replacement for repertory/materia medica confirmation.
- Treat Complete Repertory as optional later expansion; do not add it to the core canon without user approval.

### 5. General Herbal Cross-Check

Primary candidate already in the core canon:

- `Encyclopedia of Herbal Medicine`
- Author: Andrew Chevallier.
- Publisher/source page: DK.
- App use: broad herbal overview, Western/common herb properties, safety cross-checking, and general herb-family comparison.
- Research basis: DK's 2023 edition page says it includes more than 560 herbs, combines scientific research with traditional/folkloric use, and is written with safety in mind.
- URLs:
  - https://www.dk.com/us/book/9780744081794-encyclopedia-of-herbal-medicine-new-edition/
  - https://www.dk.com/us/book/9781465449818-encyclopedia-of-herbal-medicine/

Secondary candidate:

- `The Complete Herbal Tutor`
- Author: Anne McIntyre.
- App use: broader herbal principles, preparations, practice structure, and general materia medica.
- Research basis: bookseller/publisher preview sources describe it as covering theory/practice, growing/gathering/preparation, herbal prescription, and a materia medica of 150+ herbs.
- URLs:
  - https://www.allbookstores.com/The-Complete-Herbal-Tutor-The/9781911597452
  - https://health.aeonbooks.co.uk/Preview/9781911597452_PDF.pdf

Recommendation:

- Use Chevallier first because it is already user-approved.
- Keep McIntyre as an optional supplemental lane only if the user wants a broader general herbalism framework.

### 6. Case-Taking And Communication

Current usable source:

- `Organon of the Medical Art`
- Author: Samuel Hahnemann; Wenda Brewster O'Reilly edition can be used as modern edited reference metadata.
- App use: case-taking discipline, non-leading questions, totality, modalities, peculiar symptoms, careful observation.
- Research basis: NLM lists the O'Reilly edition; local processed Organon already supports aphorism-based case-taking.
- URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nlmcatalog/100969078

Possible additional candidate:

- `The Complete Repertory` / Curar project source structure.
- App use: rubric/source provenance, repertory navigation logic, remedy source referencing.
- Access status: optional metadata/software/source-structure lane only; do not treat as core without user approval.

Recommendation:

- Use Organon as the primary case-taking logic layer now.
- Add a separate communication/case-study source list later, not inside the medicine canon.
