## 2. Comparing Ayurveda / TCM / Homeopathy

Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Classical Homeopathy all organize health around patterns, but they do not use the same map.

Ayurveda often asks: What is the person's constitution, current imbalance, digestion, tissue state, and relationship to season, diet, and routine?

Traditional Chinese Medicine often asks: What pattern is showing through qi, blood, yin-yang, organ networks, channels, climate factors, and the movement of symptoms?

Classical Homeopathy often asks: What remedy picture most closely matches the person's total symptom pattern, including distinctive physical, mental, and modal features?

The overlap is that all three pay attention to the whole person. The difference is that "whole person" means something different in each system.

## 3. Explaining Overlap Between Traditions

These traditions overlap around [shared concern], but they explain it through different vocabularies.

For example, [concrete situation]. Ayurveda might describe this in terms of [Ayurvedic frame]. TCM might describe it in terms of [TCM frame]. Homeopathy might ask which individual symptom pattern is most characteristic.

The useful bridge is not to collapse the systems into one another. It is to notice that each system is trying to preserve context: body, mind, environment, timing, and lived experience.
