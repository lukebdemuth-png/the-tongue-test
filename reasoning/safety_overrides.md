# Safety Overrides

Safety overrides always come before traditional interpretation. If red flags are present, the system should route the response toward urgent clinical evaluation and avoid ranking traditional patterns until the safety issue is addressed.

## Core Rule

Red flags override:

- Ayurveda pattern interpretation
- TCM pattern interpretation
- Homeopathy remedy-picture interpretation
- Yoga or Qigong practice suggestions
- Integrative explanatory models

## Emergency Red Flags

Escalate urgently when the user reports:

- chest pain, pressure, or pain radiating to arm, jaw, back, or shoulder
- severe shortness of breath
- stroke-like symptoms: facial droop, arm weakness, speech difficulty, sudden confusion
- seizure, fainting, loss of consciousness
- sudden severe headache or worst headache of life
- severe abdominal pain, rigid abdomen, or vomiting blood
- signs of sepsis: high fever, confusion, rapid breathing, very low blood pressure
- suicidal intent, self-harm risk, or risk of harming others
- anaphylaxis: swelling of lips/tongue/throat, wheezing, rapid onset rash with breathing trouble
- severe allergic reaction after medicine, herb, food, or supplement
- severe dehydration, inability to keep fluids down
- uncontrolled bleeding
- pregnancy with severe pain, bleeding, fainting, severe headache, or high blood pressure symptoms

## High-Priority Medical Review

Recommend prompt medical review when the user reports:

- unexplained weight loss
- persistent fever
- night sweats
- blood in stool or urine
- new neurological symptoms
- new or worsening severe pain
- persistent vomiting or diarrhea
- rapidly worsening symptoms
- symptoms after head injury
- severe depression, panic, or functional collapse
- medication side effects or interactions
- symptoms in infants, frail elders, pregnancy, or immunocompromised users

## Contraindication Checks

Before any practice-oriented explanation, check for:

- pregnancy
- anticoagulant use
- immune suppression
- cancer treatment
- heart disease
- kidney or liver disease
- psychiatric crisis
- current prescriptions
- recent surgery
- implanted devices
- severe osteoporosis or injury risk for movement practices

## Output Behavior

When a safety override is triggered:

1. Name the red flag plainly.
2. Recommend urgent or prompt medical care as appropriate.
3. Avoid detailed traditional pattern ranking.
4. Offer a short bridge: `Once immediate safety is ruled out, the pattern information can be interpreted more responsibly.`

## Safe Language

Use:

- `This symptom can sometimes signal something urgent.`
- `It would be safer to get medical evaluation before interpreting this traditionally.`
- `I cannot rule out urgent causes from the information here.`

Avoid:

- diagnosing
- reassuring away red flags
- substituting traditional interpretation for urgent care
- giving herb, remedy, breathwork, movement, or supplement instructions during red-flag presentations
